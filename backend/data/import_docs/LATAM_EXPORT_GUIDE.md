# Latin America Agricultural Export Regulations to Brazil

## Argentina (Mercosul Member)
- ACE 18 (Acordo de Complementação Econômica): zero tariff for most agricultural goods
- SENASA: Servicio Nacional de Sanidad y Calidad Agroalimentaria — phytosanitary certificates
- AFIP: customs documentation
- Transit time Buenos Aires → Santos: 3-5 days (short sea shipping)
- Key advantage: Mercosul TEC exemption — II = 0% for most goods
- SISCOMEX: simplified declaration for Mercosul origins

## Colombia
- ACE 59 (Colombia-Mercosul): partial tariff reductions
- ICA (Instituto Colombiano Agropecuario): phytosanitary certificates
- DIAN: Colombian customs authority
- Transit time Cartagena → Santos: 7-9 days
- Coffee: Colombia is major exporter — high quality arabica available
- Tariff: partial reduction under ACE 59, check NCM for specific rates

## Peru
- ACE 58 (Peru-Mercosul): partial tariff reductions
- SENASA Peru: phytosanitary authority
- Transit time Callao → Santos: 10-14 days
- Coffee: Peru is growing coffee exporter, organic certification common
- Fruits: mango, avocado, blueberry — high quality export market

## Chile
- ACE 35 (Chile-Mercosul): significant tariff reductions, near zero for most agricultural
- SAG (Servicio Agrícola y Ganadero): phytosanitary certificates
- Transit time Valparaíso → Santos: 5-7 days
- Wine, fruits, salmon: main exports to Brazil
- Near-FTA status: very favorable tariff treatment

## Paraguay (Mercosul Member)
- Full Mercosul member: zero tariff for most goods
- SENAVE: phytosanitary authority
- Transit time Asunción → Santos: 2-3 days (land + short sea)
- Soybeans: major producer, competitive prices
- II = 0% for most agricultural products

## Uruguay (Mercosul Member)
- Full Mercosul member: zero tariff
- MGAP: Ministry of Agriculture phytosanitary authority
- Transit time Montevideo → Santos: 2-3 days
- Beef, dairy, wool: main exports
- II = 0% for most agricultural products

## Key Advantages of LATAM Origins vs Asia/Europe
- Shorter transit times (2-14 days vs 16-35 days)
- Mercosul/ACE agreements reduce or eliminate II
- Similar time zones — easier business communication
- Lower geopolitical risk
- Currency: mostly USD-denominated trade, some BRL/local currency options

## Brazilian Import Requirements for LATAM Products
- Certificate of Origin Form Mercosul: required for Mercosul members to claim zero II
- MAPA phytosanitary inspection: mandatory at port of entry regardless of origin
- SISCOMEX: DI filing required even for Mercosul origins (simplified channel)
- ANVISA: registration required for processed foods regardless of origin

## ACE Agreements Summary
- ACE 18 (Argentina): near-comprehensive zero tariff for agricultural goods
- ACE 35 (Chile): significant reductions, near-zero for most agriculture
- ACE 58 (Peru): partial reductions — check NCM-specific rates
- ACE 59 (Colombia): partial reductions — check NCM-specific rates
- Full Mercosul (Paraguay, Uruguay): TEC zero for most goods

## Soybeans from LATAM
- Paraguay: major soybean producer, Mercosul member — II = 0%, SENAVE certificate
- Argentina: major producer, II = 0%, SENASA certificate, AFIP export documentation
- Both require CNTBio authorization if GMO content (most LATAM soy is GMO)

## Coffee from LATAM
- Colombia: ACE 59 applies, ICA certificate, high-quality arabica
- Peru: ACE 58 applies, SENASA certificate, organic often available
- Both: MAPA inspection at Santos, ANVISA notification for processed coffee
