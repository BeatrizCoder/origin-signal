# Brazil Import Regulatory Guide

## Key Regulatory Bodies
- ANVISA: health surveillance for food, beverages, cosmetics, medicines
- MAPA: Ministry of Agriculture — controls animal/plant products
- Receita Federal: customs clearance, tariff classification
- SISCOMEX: mandatory electronic system for all import declarations

## General Import Requirements
- LI (Licença de Importação): required before shipment for controlled goods
- DI (Declaração de Importação): filed in SISCOMEX after arrival
- NCM Classification: mandatory for tariff and tax calculation

## Food & Agricultural Products (ANVISA + MAPA)
- Prior registration or notification required for most food products
- Sanitary inspection at port of entry
- Certificate of origin required
- Phytosanitary certificate for plant products (MAPA)
- Veterinary certificate for animal products (MAPA)

## Key Taxes on Imports to Brazil
- II (Imposto de Importação): varies by NCM, typically 10-35% for food
- IPI (Imposto sobre Produtos Industrializados): varies
- PIS/COFINS Importação: ~9.65% combined
- ICMS: state tax, typically 12-18%
- Total tax burden can reach 50-80% of product value

## Mercosul Agreements
- Argentina, Paraguay, Uruguay: reduced or zero II for most goods
- TEC (Tarifa Externa Comum): common external tariff for non-Mercosul

## Trade Agreements Relevant for Import
- Brasil-UE (Mercosul-EU): under ratification, may reduce tariffs
- Brasil-EUA: no comprehensive FTA, standard WTO rates apply
- Brasil-China: no FTA, standard rates + possible anti-dumping measures
- Brasil-Argentina: Mercosul TEC applies, most agri goods zero or reduced II
- Brasil-Colombia, Peru, Chile: ACE agreements under ALADI framework

## Prohibited/Restricted Imports
- GMO products: require CNTBio authorization
- Organic products: IBD or equivalent certification
- Products with phytosanitary restrictions: MAPA prior authorization

## Import Process Step by Step
1. NCM classification of product
2. Check LI requirement in SISCOMEX
3. Obtain LI approval before shipment if required
4. Shipping and transport to Brazilian port
5. Arrival — SDA/MAPA or ANVISA inspection if applicable
6. File DI (Declaração de Importação) in SISCOMEX
7. Pay II, IPI, PIS/COFINS, ICMS
8. Customs clearance (parametrização: green/yellow/red/grey channels)
9. Release of cargo

## Coffee Import Requirements
- NCM 0901.11.00 (green coffee) or 0901.21.00 (roasted)
- MAPA certificate of origin required
- Phytosanitary certificate from origin country
- II typically 10% for coffee from non-Mercosul origins
- ANVISA notification may be required for processed coffee products

## Soybean Import Requirements
- NCM 1201.90.00 (soybeans) or 1208.10.00 (soy flour)
- MAPA phytosanitary certificate mandatory
- CNTBio authorization if GMO content
- II typically 6-8% for soybeans
- Anti-dumping measures may apply for certain origins

## Fruit Import Requirements
- NCM varies by fruit type (Chapter 08)
- MAPA phytosanitary certificate and inspection required
- Cold chain documentation for temperature-sensitive products
- II typically 10-15% for fresh fruits
- ANVISA registration for processed/packaged fruits

## United States Import Notes
- No Brazil-US FTA — standard WTO MFN rates apply
- Phytosanitary requirements strictly enforced by MAPA
- Dollar-denominated contracts may require BACEN approval above thresholds

## China Import Notes
- No Brazil-China FTA — standard WTO rates
- Anti-dumping investigations active on some Chinese agricultural products
- Certificate of origin from Chinese authorities required
- ANVISA may require additional food safety documentation

## European Union Import Notes
- Mercosul-EU agreement under ratification — may reduce tariffs when in force
- EU certificates of origin accepted
- High phytosanitary standards from EU generally satisfy MAPA requirements

## Argentina Import Notes
- Mercosul member — TEC applies, most agricultural goods reduced or zero II
- MAPA phytosanitary requirements still apply
- SISCOMEX LI may still be required depending on product

## Colombia, Peru, Chile Import Notes
- ACE agreements under ALADI — preferential tariff rates for many products
- Colombia: ACE 24; Peru: ACE 58; Chile: ACE 35
- Phytosanitary certificates required for all agricultural products
