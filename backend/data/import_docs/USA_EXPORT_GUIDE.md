# US Agricultural Export Regulations to Brazil

## Key US Regulatory Bodies for Exports
- USDA APHIS: Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service — phytosanitary certificates
- FDA: Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA) compliance required
- USDA AMS: Agricultural Marketing Service — grading and standards

## US Coffee Exports to Brazil
- Coffee re-export: US is a processor/re-exporter, not primary producer
- Requires FDA registration of food facilities
- FSMA Prior Notice: mandatory 8 hours before arrival for food products
- Certificate of Origin: required for Brazilian customs

## US Soybeans to Brazil
- Brazil is a net exporter — US soy imports to Brazil are rare
- When applicable: USDA phytosanitary certificate required
- GMO declaration mandatory (Brazil CNTBio authorization needed)
- APHIS export certificate for plant products

## US Fruits to Brazil
- MAPA phytosanitary requirements apply upon entry
- Cold treatment certificates for specific fruits (apples, grapes, citrus)
- USDA APHIS export certificate mandatory
- Pesticide residue compliance with Brazilian MAPA limits

## Trade Context Brazil-USA
- No comprehensive FTA — standard WTO rates apply
- Anti-dumping measures: some US agricultural products subject to Brazilian anti-dumping
- Exchange rate risk: USD/BRL volatility significant for cost planning
- SISCOMEX: standard II rates apply (no preferential treatment)

## Logistics USA → Brazil
- Miami/New York → Porto de Santos: ~14-18 days
- Port of New Orleans → Porto de Santos: ~16-20 days
- Los Angeles → Porto de Santos: ~22-28 days (via Panama Canal)

## FDA FSMA Requirements
- Foreign Supplier Verification Program (FSVP): Brazilian importer must verify US supplier compliance
- Prior Notice: submitted via FDA system at least 8 hours before arrival by sea
- Food facility registration: US exporter must be registered with FDA

## USDA APHIS Export Certificates
- Phytosanitary Certificate: issued per shipment for plant products
- Additional Declaration: specific pest-free statements as required by MAPA
- Valid only for the specific shipment and destination

## Brazilian Import Taxes for US Products
- II (Imposto de Importação): typically 10-35% — no preferential rate (no FTA)
- IPI: varies by product
- PIS/COFINS Importação: ~9.65%
- ICMS: 12-18% depending on state
- Total burden can reach 50-80% of CIF value

## Anti-Dumping Risk — USA to Brazil
- Some processed food products from the US have been subject to Brazilian anti-dumping investigation
- Check CAMEX resolutions before contracting for any manufactured/processed goods
- Agricultural commodities generally not subject to anti-dumping

## Documentation Checklist for US → Brazil Agricultural Imports
1. Commercial invoice (Portuguese or bilingual)
2. Packing list
3. Bill of lading or airway bill
4. USDA APHIS phytosanitary certificate
5. FDA Prior Notice confirmation
6. Certificate of Origin
7. LI (Licença de Importação) if required
8. CNTBio authorization if GMO content
