# Chinese Agricultural Export Regulations to Brazil

## Key Chinese Regulatory Bodies
- GACC (General Administration of Customs): controls all Chinese exports
- SAMR: State Administration for Market Regulation — quality standards
- MARA: Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Affairs

## Chinese Products to Brazil — Key Requirements
- GACC registration: Chinese exporters must be registered with GACC
- CIQ Certificate: China Inspection and Quarantine certificate required
- Phytosanitary certificate: issued by GACC for plant products
- Certificate of Origin: Form A for GSP or standard CO

## Soybeans (China → Brazil — rare, Brazil is exporter)
- Not commercially significant flow
- If applicable: GACC phytosanitary + MAPA authorization at entry

## Processed Food Products (China → Brazil)
- ANVISA registration mandatory before commercialization in Brazil
- Chinese food additives may differ from Brazilian ANVISA permitted list
- Labeling in Portuguese mandatory (Lei 8.078/1990)
- Higher ANVISA scrutiny for Chinese processed foods

## Trade Context Brazil-China
- No comprehensive FTA — but strong bilateral trade relationship
- Brazil-China trade agreement under discussion (2024-2025)
- Anti-dumping: several Chinese product categories subject to Brazilian anti-dumping duties
- Currency: CNY/BRL — often settled in USD
- ICMS: varies by state, typically 12-18%

## Geopolitical Risk Factors
- US-China trade tensions may affect supply chain routing
- Chinese export controls on strategic goods (not typically agricultural)
- Port congestion in Shanghai/Qingdao can delay departure

## Logistics China → Brazil
- Shanghai → Porto de Santos: ~28-35 days
- Qingdao → Porto de Santos: ~30-37 days
- Shenzhen → Porto de Santos: ~32-38 days

## Key Brazilian Requirements for Chinese Imports
- Higher customs inspection rate (canal vermelho more likely)
- Anti-dumping investigation risk for manufactured goods
- INMETRO certification for some processed products

## GACC Registration Requirements
- Chinese food exporters must be registered in GACC system
- Registration number must appear on all export documentation
- GACC conducts pre-export inspections for regulated products

## Brazilian Import Taxes for Chinese Products
- II (Imposto de Importação): standard WTO rates, typically 10-35% for food
- IPI: varies by NCM classification
- PIS/COFINS Importação: ~9.65%
- ICMS: 12-18% depending on destination state
- Anti-dumping surcharge: may add 10-50% on top of II for affected categories

## Anti-Dumping Risk — China to Brazil
- CAMEX maintains active anti-dumping measures on several Chinese product categories
- Food processing equipment, packaging materials, and some processed foods affected
- Check MDIC/CAMEX database before contracting
- Agricultural raw commodities generally lower risk than processed goods

## Documentation Checklist for China → Brazil Agricultural Imports
1. Commercial invoice (Portuguese or bilingual)
2. Packing list
3. Bill of lading
4. GACC phytosanitary/inspection certificate
5. Certificate of Origin (Form A if GSP eligible)
6. ANVISA registration confirmation (for processed foods)
7. LI (Licença de Importação) via SISCOMEX
8. CNTBio authorization if GMO content
9. Portuguese labeling compliance declaration
